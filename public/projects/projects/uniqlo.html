UNIQLO US Digital Commerce

I worked at UNIQLO as a frontend developer for 7 years. Over that span I participated in two replatforming projects, first moving from Adobe's CQ5 to a Salesforce Reference Architecture (SFRA) site, and most recently migrating from SFRA to a bespoke CMS developed in house.

In both projects myself and the developer under me worked to massage content into its expected layouts, and with the latest launch we put extensive time into taking the site from what was essentially two separate CMSs and condensing everything into a single responsive code base.

In order to streamline the work we had done, I constructed a scaffolding tool that allowed us to spin up projects quickly and work on them in an environment that closely matched that on our staging and production builds. I worked with a co-developer and the design team to unify the styling employed on the site. While it wasn't a complete design system, our implementation allowed for accelerated design and development processes, reducing expended effort and the potential for mistakes.
