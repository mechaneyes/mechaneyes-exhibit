Formalism

I picked up a camera in 1994, and was immediately enamored. As my very first print was taking shape in the developer tray, a guy next to me took a look and and reacted to it in the same way I had myself. He got the joke! Being painfully shy back then this opened up the door to an alternate communication channel. I could tell jokes without uttering a word.

Stepping things up, I left the Best Coast to study photography at Parsons School of Design.

I still shoot every day.

The work below focuses on formal elements of visual art: line, shape, space, color and texture. I work in a number of loosely coupled mediums, but this collection of images ties directly to the foundation upon which most of what I do is built.
